Gundam, a famous Japanese media franchise by Yoshiyuki Tomino and Sunrise. It first appeared in 1979 as the television series Mobile Suit Gundam. The shows revolve around gigantic robots referred to as Mobile Suits with a special focus on the elite ones labeled "Gundams." Because of its deep portrayal of war, politics and human nature, Gundam has become a cultural phenomenon with its expansion into multiple TV shows, movies, manga and games. It is the uniqueness and variegation in design of those Mobile Suits coupled with attractive story lines that have kept Gundam in the list of all-time favorite animes for fans all over the globe.

Gunpla is a clipped compound word from "Gundam Plastic Model," which refers to plastic model kits depicting the Mobile Suits from the Gundam series. Building Gunpla consists of piecing together parts obtained by breaking them off plastic runners. Those who handle these models have to cut the parts carefully from the runners, trim any excess plastic, and then fit everything according to the directions given. Many fans do more than just snap together their models; they paint them, apply decals, make custom modifications for added detail and appearance. It is through this act of creation that one can stamp one's own individuality on the finished model; no two completed ones are ever quite alike. The pursuit combines getting one's hands dirty with a display of ingenuity all while reveling in knowledge about this mythic universe.
